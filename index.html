Your Workout Journey

Every great workout follows a structure. Our beginner-friendly flow takes you from warm-up to cool-down, ensuring you get the most out of each session while staying safe.

1

Warm-Up

5-7 minutes

Prepare your body for exercise with dynamic movements that increase blood flow and loosen your muscles.

  • Light jogging in place or marching
  • Arm circles (forward and backward)
  • Leg swings (front-to-back, side-to-side)
  • Torso twists and hip circles

Tip: You should feel slightly warmer and your breathing should increase slightly. If you're still cold, add another minute.

2

Strength Block A: Upper Body

8-10 minutes

Build upper body strength with pushing and pulling movements. Focus on controlled motions rather than speed.

  • Push-ups (or knee push-ups): 3 sets × 8-12 reps
  • Dumbbell rows (or resistance band rows): 3 sets × 10 reps each arm
  • Shoulder press (light weight or bodyweight): 3 sets × 10 reps

Tip: If push-ups are too hard, start against a wall. There's no shame in modifications—that's how you build up!

3

Strength Block B: Lower Body & Core

8-10 minutes

Strengthen your legs and core—the foundation of all movement. These exercises improve balance and everyday activities.

  • Bodyweight squats: 3 sets × 12-15 reps
  • Lunges (alternating): 3 sets × 10 reps each leg
  • Glute bridges: 3 sets × 12 reps
  • Plank hold: 3 sets × 20-30 seconds

Tip: For squats, imagine sitting back into a chair. Keep your knees tracking over your toes, not caving inward.

4

Cool-Down & Stretch

5-7 minutes

Gradually bring your heart rate down and stretch your muscles to improve flexibility and reduce soreness.

  • Slow walking or gentle marching (1-2 minutes)
  • Standing quad stretch (30 seconds each leg)
  • Hamstring stretch (30 seconds each leg)
  • Chest opener stretch (30 seconds)
  • Deep breathing exercises (1 minute)

Tip: Hold each stretch without bouncing. Breathe deeply and let your muscles relax into each position.
